Recipient Spotlight: Maria's Story

Before Donations: Maria, a single mother of three, struggled to provide nutritious meals for her children due to financial hardships.

After Receiving Donations: Thanks to the generous food donations, Maria's family now enjoys a variety of healthy foods. The children are doing better in school, and Maria has started a small home-based business to support her family.

"The food donations have been a lifeline for us. It's more than just the meals; it's the hope and support that come with them. We are deeply grateful." - Maria

Impact Achieved: With the help of donors like you, over 500 families in Maria's community have received essential food support this year.

Calculate Your Food Waste

Did you know that the average American wastes 219 pounds of food each year? Use our calculator to estimate your food waste and learn how you can reduce it.

Your Food Waste:

You waste pounds of food annually.

To reduce your food waste, consider the following tips:

  • Shop Smart: Plan your meals and make a shopping list to avoid buying more than you need. Frequent, smaller shopping trips can help ensure that you use all the food you buy.
  • Store Food Correctly: Proper storage can greatly extend the life of your food. For example, keep potatoes, tomatoes, garlic, cucumbers, and onions at room temperature, and separate ethylene-producing fruits like bananas and avocados from other produce to prevent premature spoilage.
  • Learn to Preserve: Pickling, fermenting, and freezing are great ways to extend the shelf life of foods. These methods have been used for thousands of years and can help you save food that might otherwise go to waste.
  • Take Inventory: Regularly check what you have in your pantry, fridge, and freezer to avoid overbuying and to use up items before they expire.
  • Create a Meal Plan: Having a meal plan helps you use what you have and reduces the chances of food going unused and eventually wasted.
  • Use Leftovers Creatively: Think of ways to repurpose leftovers into new meals. For example, use leftover vegetables in a stir-fry or soup.
  • Compost: Composting inedible scraps turns waste into a resource and reduces the amount of garbage sent to landfills.
  • Educate Yourself and Others: Understanding the impact of food waste and sharing knowledge can help create a community that values and practices waste reduction.
